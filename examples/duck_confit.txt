Duck confit with cherry gastrique and wilted spinach.

The duck legs are cured overnight in coarse salt, garlic, and fresh thyme,
then slow-cooked submerged in their own rendered fat at 180°F for six hours
until the meat is silky and yields from the bone.

The cherry gastrique begins with sugar caramelized to a deep amber, then
deglazed with red wine vinegar and reduced with fresh Bing cherries and a
splash of port wine until thick and glossy.

Wilted spinach is briefly sautéed in butter with shallots and finished with
a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.

Served with crispy potato galette and a garnish of fresh thyme sprigs.

Tonkotsu miso ramen with chashu pork and soft-boiled egg.

The tonkotsu broth is made from pork bones roasted until golden, then simmered
for twelve hours with ginger, garlic, and scallion. The long simmer extracts
collagen and gelatin, giving the broth a rich, milky, sticky body.

White miso paste is whisked into the hot broth at the end, adding a deep
fermented umami note and a touch of sweetness. Seasoned with soy sauce and
a small amount of toasted sesame oil.

Chashu pork belly is rolled, tied, and braised in soy sauce, mirin, sake,
and sugar until the fat is translucent and the meat pulls apart. Sliced thin
and seared before serving.

Soft-boiled eggs are marinated overnight in soy sauce, mirin, and sake
until the whites are mahogany-colored and the yolk is jammy.

Assembled with fresh ramen noodles, bamboo shoots, nori, sliced scallions,
toasted sesame seeds, and a drizzle of chili oil.
